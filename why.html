Why arent we using renewable energy?

Everyday life in the 21st century is vitally dependant on electricity.
Without electricity, the world around us would collapse. However, the
most common and used methods of generating electricity require nonrenewable
energy sources. They are mostly fossil fuels which include oil, natural gas
, coal, and much more. Nonrenewable energy sources will eventually be depleted
if they are continually used at the current scale. Nonrenewable energy sources
also release harmful byproducts into the atmosphere when burned. Byproducts
include carbon dioxide, carbon monoxide, Sulfur dioxide, Nitrogen oxide, lead
and much more. Some byproducts increase greenhouse gases in the atmosphere,
which directly contributes to global warming. Other byproducts can cause ocean
acidification, poor air quality, and many other negative effects on Earth. Even
though there are many methods of generating electricity that do not require fossil
fuels (renewable energy) many of the methods prove to be inefficient or not always
able to function. Wind turbines can only reach around 40% efficiency and only during
high-frequency wind. Most solar panels can only convert 14- 20 % of all the energy
they harvest
