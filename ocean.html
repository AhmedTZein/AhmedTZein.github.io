Ocean Energy

The ocean has an endless supply of kinetic energy. Whether itâ€™s by waves, currents, or tides,
there are vast amounts of energy that can be harvested to power the world, but with the right
technology. The ocean alone absorbs 75% of the solar energy received by the earth and from
that energy, there are waves, currents, and wind which can all be harnessed and converted into
usable energy. Other energy sources that can be harnessed from the ocean include tides. Tides,
the rise and fall of sea levels created by the gravitational effects of the sun, moon, and the
earth's rotation, were used for their kinetic energy from their change in height and movement
to gyrate mills in ancient Europe and are now used to generate electricity in many places like
Britain and Canada's Bay of Fundy. Today tidal energy is harnessed by turbines, which are devices
that use the water flow to rotate a rotor and then by using a hydroelectric generator the turbine
is able to convert the mechanical energy into electrical energy.
